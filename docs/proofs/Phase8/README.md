### 0.3 Assessment of Individual Predictions

**Prediction 8.1.3 (Three Generations):**
- **Status:** Post-hoc explanation of known fact
- **Value:** Shows the framework is *consistent* with N_gen = 3
- **Honest claim:** "The stella geometry ACCOMMODATES three generations" not "PREDICTS"
- **What would falsify:** Discovery of 4th generation (would falsify the geometric necessity claim)

**Prediction 8.4.2 (θ₁₃ = 8.54°):**
- **Status:** Post-hoc fit with tuned coefficients
- **Concern:** The formula sin(θ₁₃) = (λ/φ)(1 + λ/5 + λ²/2) has coefficients 1/5 and 1/2 that appear retrofitted
- **Defense:** The coefficients have geometric meaning (5-fold symmetry, 2 tetrahedra), but this is circumstantial
- **Honest claim:** "An elegant formula exists" not "A first-principles prediction"

**Prediction 8.2.1 (QGP Phase Coherence):**
- **Status:** The most genuinely novel testable prediction
- **Prediction:** Coherence length ξ ~ 0.44847 fm in quark-gluon plasma
- **Testability:** ALICE and STAR experiments at RHIC/LHC
- **Confidence:** ~40% (framework-internal estimate)
- **Honest claim:** This is the best candidate for genuine falsification

> **Most predictions are post-hoc explanations** of known physics, providing consistency checks rather than novel tests.

> **The framework's value is primarily organizational:** It connects QCD, gravity, and quantum mechanics through a common geometric structure, even if individual predictions are not strongly constrained.
